About Me

I am a computational and theoretical researcher at MIT, specializing in development and implementation of advanced numerical techniques and scientific softwares for quantum simulations. With 8+ years of experience in high-performance and parallel computing, I have designed and optimized scalable softwares and numerical libraries using Python and C++ to push the boundaries of quantum simulation capabilities. My work leverages parallel computing architectures to significantly enhance computational efficiency and performance.

Throughout my career, I have successfully developed high-performance computing solutions that address complex scientific and technological challenges, enabling more efficient and accurate quantum simulations.

I am passionate about leveraging my unique computing and numerical skills to create practical, impactful application and drive innovation. Whether it's developing cutting-edge algorithms or optimizing existing systems for better performance, I am dedicated to making meaningful contributions to the community.
